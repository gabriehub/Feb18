Who I Am

Psychology meets purpose-driven product thinking

My background is in psychology and behavior design ‚Äî the study of why people do what they do, and how systems can be shaped to guide better decisions. I'm now applying that lens to sustainability at Stanford's Doerr School.

What draws me to Inversa and specifically to the Origin platform is the psychology-oriented design challenge at its core: how do you tell the narrative of a lionfish caught off a coral reef in the Yucatan in a way that moves the consumer? How do you motivate behavior change at the moment someone is deciding whether to buy?

I work with the Fogg Behavior Model, wise interventions, and Donella Meadows' leverage points ‚Äî frameworks for understanding where small changes in a system produce shifts in behavior. That toolkit feels directly relevant to what Origin is trying to do.

The Stanford practicum is specifically designed to be a co-created partnership. We define the project scope together, based on what Inversa actually needs right now.

  • 80+ hours of project work with the partner organization

  • A genuine, mutually co-developed project scope ‚Äî not busywork
  • Opportunities to collaborate with staff and attend team meetings
  • A sustainability challenge I can meaningfully engage with
  • No payment required for the 80 core hours (though travel/lodging costs can be covered)
  • Optional: the project can be structured as part of a longer paid engagement

  • The practicum is a capstone academic experience ‚Äî I bring Stanford rigor and frameworks, not just task completion
